•  24 y.o, lives in Ho Chi Minh city, Vietnam

•  Income: ~500 usd / month*

Characteristics

•  Loves reading, travelling and taking photos

Purposes for

Online Shopping

•  Window Shopping - to discover new products about books, cosmetics, fashion, health

•  To search for current interested products

•  To compare relevant products

•  To catch up with current promotions

•  To make purchasing order

•  To manage personal information and orders

Ann - 24 y.o

Persona image is credited from HongHanh.Bookaholic

After I created the Hypothetical Persona, I proceeded to create an online survey.

My 2 goals for the survey include:

1. To collect an adequate number of real customers for the face-to-face interviews.

2. To understand target customers' interests and tendencies when they shop online.

Critical characteristics that I will pick as the target are based on the Persona, but also:

•  They are smart-phone users (mandatory)

•  They have made online purchases at least 1 time within 6 recent months (mandatory)

•  They used to shop on Tiki.vn

•  They know certain E-commerce websites in Vietnam

There were 40 responses in which I ultimately chose 15 interviewees.

As I recruited 15 interviewees, I decided to arrange into 3 interview sessions, in which I will interview 5 people per session.

Along with the questions, I also made this Process Flow from the 3 scenarios.

The Interviewee column will be blank for checkmarks if the user performed the task during the interview.

To record the interview's notes, I prepared this note-taking template to quickly jot down important points I perceived during the interview. Each interview was expected to last from 15 to 25 minutes.

2.5 - Real User Interviews

Customer feedback is a prerequisite content that potential customers are interested in. Hence, encouraging customers to give feedback will stimulate purchases from potential customers and interaction between users and the app.


Product image is the second most important factor because the customers want to see how the product looks like before buying.


The product information should be as detailed as possible (in terms of color, size, model size, components, purchase instructions). It will help buyers feel more confident in their purchases.

Users are only interested in the content of their favourite products. Other items will be completely ignored. Therefore, the content of the product should be personalized to the users' interests.

Cataloging by listing will be unappealing to customers. However, the image grid view makes the application load slower and requires higher capacity.

When the user search with filtering function, we should display all critical options about the product type.

There should be a function for users to review the previously viewed products.

Funtional icons should be easy to understand.  It is best to write annotations below the icon or use text to help the users perform the functions quickly.

In the product category, there should have inspiring Product Lists like "New & Hot" - "Top Sellers" - "Top 100" to stimulate users to explore products in the list when there is no real need.

Too many promotion banners on the small screen will make it hard to spot the user's attention

ABOUT THE PROJECT

Starting from selling books online in 2010, Tiki.vn has become one of the most favorite B2C companies in Vietnam. In their e-commerce website, Tiki has featured more than 300,000 products in under 10 product categories.

In 2015, Tiki has developed and launched their mobile app to target mobile shoppers. This project is about redesign the current Tiki's mobile app to improve their customer experience while also will increase the customer's conversion rate.

Mobile App Prototype

Sponsored by Tiki, "Amazing UX - The Award for Exceptional UX Designers" is a UX Design competition across Vietnam, started from February to June 2016. The competition aims to be a playground for all the designers to produce better UX solutions for the mobile market of top tech companies. 

In this competition, among other apps, I have chosen Tiki to work on the app improvement.

TL;DR: To Long; Didn't Read

The Process

1.1 - Research and analyze Customer Behavior for online shopping

Before working on the mobile version, I have done a Mini UX Review with their current website to understand their process. (However, it will not be included here as I want to focus on the mobile only). After I passed the competition's first stage for the UX Review, I continued to do real user interviews. Selecting target customers were based on the analyzed persona. I conducted 3 interview sessions with 15 interviewees. Then, I summarized the results to draw the customer experience map up together with their desired functions which were based on their painpoints. My final deliverables includes the interview report, UX documents and interactive prototype.  For the competition's result, I made it to the top 10 finalists among 400 participants through 3 competed rounds.
